I'd appreciate your feedback on a new Ramachandran analysis focused database built from the top2018Full 70% Homology Residue Level filtered dataset.

What it contains: Per-residue backbone geometry (phi, psi, omega, tau), sidechain torsion (chi1), Ramachandran classifications, and DSSP secondary structure for 2,405,322 quality-filtered protein residues from 12,125 chains in 11,843 high-resolution protein structures. The source data is the Richardson Lab top2018 dataset (Williams et al., 2021, full-filtered, 70% homology).

What's new: The measurements are computed on "ersatz" full-structure PDB files that preserve multi-chain context for correct DSSP secondary structure assignments — something the original single-chain pruned files cannot provide. NQH sidechain flip corrections from Reduce are applied using the three-point hinge rotation method, matching the published Zenodo coordinates. Fragment-aware post-filtering ensures no measurement depends on coordinates from quality-filtered residues.

Links:

  • Zenodo dataset: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19029946
  • Pipeline code and documentation: https://github.com/prisant/richardson-dataset-curation
  • Measurement tool: https://github.com/prisant/pydangle-biopython

The Zenodo deposit includes a detailed issues document (top2018_issues.md) describing every problem encountered during preparation and how it was resolved. The pipeline is fully reproducible from publicly available sources.

I'd welcome any comments on the methodology, the data, or the documentation.
